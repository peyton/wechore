Privacy Policy

WeChore Privacy Policy

WeChore is designed as a local-first household chore app. This policy explains what the app handles, which Apple system features are optional, and how to contact support.

Contact

For privacy questions, email privacy@wechore.peyton.app. For app support, email support@wechore.peyton.app. For security reports, email security@wechore.peyton.app. General contact goes to contact@wechore.peyton.app.

Data WeChore Handles

WeChore can store household names, member display names, optional phone or FaceTime handles, chore titles, notes, assignees, due dates, status, reminder history, in-app messages, and accepted or dismissed suggestions.

Local Storage

WeChore stores app data in the app sandbox on your device using SwiftData. Removing the app can remove local app data from that device.

CloudKit Sharing

When household sharing is enabled, WeChore uses Apple CloudKit and iCloud sharing through member Apple IDs. WeChore does not operate its own sync server for household data.

Messages And Suggestions

WeChore suggests chores only from messages typed inside WeChore. It does not read Messages app conversations. Suggestion extraction runs on device with Apple text APIs and each suggestion requires review before it becomes a chore.

Notifications And Handoff

Local notifications are scheduled only after Apple notification permission is allowed. Voice and text nudges can open FaceTime Audio, Phone, or Messages using Apple system handoff.

Ads, Tracking, And Analytics

WeChore does not include advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or cross-app tracking.

Retention And Deletion

App data remains on your device unless you delete it, remove the app, or remove related iCloud data through Apple settings.
